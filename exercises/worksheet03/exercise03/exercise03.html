  • List of Transformation Matrices Used:
    • In Part 1:
      • Translation Matrix: Used to move the cube within the world coordinate system.
      • Rotation Matrices: Rotated the cube around the Y-axis and then the X-axis to achieve the isometric view.
    • In Part 2:
      • Translation Matrices: Applied to position the cubes differently in the scene for various perspective views.
      • Scaling Matrix: Scaled the cube to adjust its size in the viewport.
      • Perspective Matrix: Set up the pinhole camera model with a specific field of view to create a perspective projection.
      • Look-at Matrix: Defined the position of the camera and where it is looking, affecting the orientation of the cubes in the scene.
  • Current Transformation Matrix (CTM) Formulas:
    • For Part 1:
      • The CTM was a combination of rotation and translation matrices applied to the cube vertices. The formula was: CTM = rotateX * rotateY * translate.
    • For Part 2 (for different perspectives):
      • 1-point Perspective: The formula was CTM = translate * scalem * perspective * look-at. This combination of matrices positioned the cube, scaled it, applied perspective projection, and set the camera view.
      • 2 and 3-point Perspectives: The formula was extended with an additional translation matrix: CTM = translate * scalem * perspective * translate * lookAt. This additional translation allowed for more complex positioning of the cubes in the scene, creating different classical perspective views.
