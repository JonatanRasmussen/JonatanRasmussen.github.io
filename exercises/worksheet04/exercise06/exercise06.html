  • Question 1: Phong Lighting vs. Phong Shading
    • Phong Lighting: A model for light reflection, including ambient, diffuse, and specular light components.
    • Phong Shading: A technique for interpolating surface normals to compute pixel colors.
  • Question 2: Comparison of Shading Techniques
    • Flat Shading: Single color per polygon. Efficient, but can appear blocky.
    • Gouraud Shading: Interpolates lighting across vertices for smoother shading than flat shading.
    • Phong Shading: Most realistic shading, interpolates normals for pixel-level lighting, but computationally intensive.
  • Question 3: Directional Light vs. Point Light
    • Directional Light: Parallel light rays, simulates distant sources like the sun.
    • Point Light: Accounts for distance, enabling dynamic lighting effects.
  • Question 4: Impact of Eye Position on Shading
    • The observer's position affects specular highlights in the Phong model.
  • Question 5: Effect of Zero Specular Term
    • Eliminates specular light, leaving only ambient and diffuse lighting.
  • Question 6: Increasing Shininess Exponent
    • Increases sharpness and decreases the size of specular highlights.
  • Question 7: Coordinate Space for Lighting Calculations
    • Lighting calculations performed in the camera or eye coordinate space.
